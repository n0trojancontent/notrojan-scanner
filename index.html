🛡️What is No Trojan®?
No Trojan® is a cultural movement and standard that measures influence and promotes personal responsibility for healthy, sustainable communities. We believe private actions and decisions of individuals and corporations in pursuit of financial and economic goals, at the expense of the public welfare, are socially irresponsible. No Trojan is a call to question popular culture, analyze conventional wisdom, evaluate the status quo, and do only that which is for the greater good. No Trojan® is a cultural influence scoring and detection system that evaluates and measures content against three established standards (EOS, SI, and GC) to detect harmful cultural influences. Like antivirus software — it scans content for malicious influences and identifies memetic threats the way antivirus scans for malicious code.

Ethical Operating System (EOS)
INDIVIDUAL GOVERNANCE · MIND / BODY / SPIRIT
Every person functions as an operating system — processing information, messaging, incentives, and values. Brodie's memetics science confirms: ideas spread like viruses through individual operating systems and into communities. EOS asks — does this content strengthen or weaken the individual's capacity for discernment, agency, and responsible decision-making? "As within, so without."
Agency
Reinforces self-determination and authorship of one's own choices.
Integrity
Aligns behavior with principled, long-term values over short-term reward.
Discernment
Encourages critical evaluation of information before adoption.
Responsibility
Models accountability for choices and their consequences.
Character
Cultivates virtue, honesty, courage, and discipline as aspirational.
Mind / Body / Spirit
Addresses the whole person — thoughts, behaviors, values, identity, and spirit.

ML
Media Literacy — The Analytical Method
THE LENS · NOT A PILLAR · UNESCO / NAMLE / WEF / CORTLAND
Media literacy is not scored separately — it is the diagnostic lens applied in every scan. UNESCO defines MIL as "the ability to access, analyze, evaluate, create, and act using all forms of communication." The five core questions below surface both explicit and implicit programming in any piece of content.
Q1
Who created this message?
Authorship, institutional incentives, who benefits from distribution.
Q2
What techniques attract attention?
Emotion, status, shock, humor, controversy — the hook mechanism.
Q3
How might people interpret this differently?
Differential impact across communities, ages, vulnerabilities.
Q4
What values and behaviors are promoted?
Norms being normalized — explicitly and implicitly modeled.
Q5
Why is this message being sent?
Economic, political, cultural, or social incentives driving production.
